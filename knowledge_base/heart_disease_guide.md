# Heart Disease: Complete Health Guide

## Overview
Cardiovascular disease (CVD) is the leading cause of death globally, responsible for approximately 17.9 million deaths per year. It encompasses a range of conditions affecting the heart and blood vessels, including coronary artery disease (CAD), heart failure, arrhythmias, and valvular heart disease. Early detection and lifestyle modification can dramatically reduce risk.

## Types of Heart Disease

### Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)
- Buildup of atherosclerotic plaques in coronary arteries narrowing blood flow to heart muscle
- Leads to angina (stable or unstable) and myocardial infarction (heart attack)
- Most common form of heart disease; accounts for the majority of cardiac deaths

### Heart Failure
- Heart cannot pump sufficient blood to meet body's needs
- Classified by ejection fraction: HFrEF (EF <40%), HFmrEF (40–49%), HFpEF (EF ≥50%)
- Causes: CAD, hypertension, cardiomyopathy, valvular disease

### Arrhythmias
- Abnormal heart rhythm: too fast (tachycardia), too slow (bradycardia), or irregular
- Atrial fibrillation (AFib): most common sustained arrhythmia; increases stroke risk 5×
- Ventricular fibrillation: life-threatening emergency; requires immediate defibrillation

### Valvular Heart Disease
- Stenosis (valve narrowing) or regurgitation (leaking valve)
- Aortic stenosis, mitral regurgitation most common in adults
- Can lead to heart failure if untreated

### Hypertensive Heart Disease
- Chronic high blood pressure causes left ventricular hypertrophy and diastolic dysfunction
- A major risk factor for heart failure and sudden cardiac death

## Risk Factors

### Non-Modifiable
- Age: Men ≥45 years, Women ≥55 years (post-menopausal)
- Male sex (though women's risk increases significantly post-menopause)
- Family history: first-degree relative with CVD before age 55 (men) or 65 (women)
- Genetic predisposition: familial hypercholesterolemia, inherited cardiomyopathies

### Modifiable
- **Hypertension**: Systolic ≥130 mmHg or diastolic ≥80 mmHg
- **Dyslipidemia**: High LDL, low HDL, elevated triglycerides
- **Smoking**: Increases CVD risk 2–4×; toxins damage endothelium, promote clot formation
- **Diabetes mellitus**: 2–4× higher CVD risk; hyperglycemia damages blood vessels
- **Obesity**: BMI ≥30 increases risk through hypertension, insulin resistance, dyslipidemia
- **Physical inactivity**: Sedentary lifestyle linked to 35% higher CVD risk
- **Unhealthy diet**: High saturated fat, sodium, sugar, low fruits and vegetables
- **Excessive alcohol**: >2 drinks/day for men, >1 for women raises blood pressure
- **Chronic stress**: Elevates cortisol, promotes inflammation and clotting
- **Sleep apnea**: Causes nocturnal hypoxia, hypertension, and atrial fibrillation

## Symptoms

### Angina (Chest Pain from CAD)
- Stable angina: Predictable chest pressure/tightness on exertion, relieved by rest or nitroglycerin
- Unstable angina: Chest pain at rest or with minimal activity; medical emergency
- Character: squeezing, pressure, heaviness, tightness
- Radiation: jaw, left arm, shoulder, back, neck

### Heart Attack (Myocardial Infarction)
- Sudden onset chest pain/pressure lasting >20 minutes, not relieved by nitroglycerin
- Associated symptoms: sweating (diaphoresis), nausea, vomiting, shortness of breath
- Women often present atypically: fatigue, indigestion, jaw/back pain, nausea (without chest pain)
- STEMI vs NSTEMI: ST-segment elevation on ECG indicates complete coronary occlusion

### Heart Failure
- Dyspnea (shortness of breath), especially on exertion or when lying flat (orthopnea)
- Paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea (waking up breathless at night)
- Peripheral edema (swelling in ankles and legs)
- Fatigue, reduced exercise tolerance
- Rapid weight gain from fluid retention (>2 lbs/day or 5 lbs/week)
- Ascites, hepatomegaly in right-sided heart failure

### Arrhythmias
- Palpitations (awareness of heartbeat), skipped beats
- Dizziness, lightheadedness, syncope (fainting)
- Shortness of breath, chest discomfort
- In AFib: irregular pulse, may be asymptomatic

## Diagnostic Tests

### Electrocardiogram (ECG/EKG)
- 12-lead ECG: detects ST changes, arrhythmias, left ventricular hypertrophy, bundle branch blocks
- ST elevation in STEMI; ST depression in NSTEMI/unstable angina
- Q waves: sign of prior MI

### Blood Tests (Cardiac Biomarkers)
- **Troponin I/T**: Gold standard for myocardial injury; rises within 3–4 hours of MI, peaks 12–24h
- **High-sensitivity troponin**: Allows rapid rule-in/rule-out protocols (0/1h or 0/2h)
- **BNP / NT-proBNP**: Elevated in heart failure; correlates with severity
- **CK-MB**: Less specific than troponin; used for reinfarction detection
- **CRP / hsCRP**: Inflammation marker; elevated CRP associated with higher CVD risk

### Imaging
- **Echocardiogram**: Assesses ejection fraction, wall motion, valve function, pericardial effusion
- **Stress test**: Exercise ECG or pharmacological stress with imaging (echo or nuclear)
- **Coronary angiography (cardiac catheterization)**: Gold standard for CAD diagnosis; allows intervention (PCI)
- **CT angiography (CTA)**: Non-invasive coronary artery imaging; calcium scoring
- **Cardiac MRI**: Best for cardiomyopathy, pericardial disease, viability assessment

### Lipid Panel (Target Values)
| Parameter | Optimal | Borderline | High Risk |
|-----------|---------|------------|-----------|
| Total Cholesterol | <200 mg/dL | 200–239 | ≥240 |
| LDL Cholesterol | <100 mg/dL (<70 with CVD) | 130–159 | ≥160 |
| HDL Cholesterol | ≥60 mg/dL (protective) | 40–59 | <40 (men), <50 (women) |
| Triglycerides | <150 mg/dL | 150–199 | ≥200 |
| Non-HDL Cholesterol | <130 mg/dL (<100 with CVD) | — | — |

### Blood Pressure Targets
- Normal: <120/80 mmHg
- Elevated: 120–129/80 mmHg
- Stage 1 HTN: 130–139/80–89 mmHg
- Stage 2 HTN: ≥140/90 mmHg
- Hypertensive crisis: >180/120 mmHg
- **Target for most CVD patients**: <130/80 mmHg

## Key Clinical Scores
- **HEART Score**: Predicts 30-day MACE in chest pain patients (History, ECG, Age, Risk factors, Troponin)
- **GRACE Score**: 6-month mortality risk in ACS patients
- **CHA₂DS₂-VASc**: Stroke risk in AFib patients; guides anticoagulation therapy
- **Framingham Risk Score / PCE**: 10-year ASCVD risk; guides statin therapy

## Treatment

### Lifestyle Modifications
- Heart-healthy diet: Mediterranean or DASH diet; limit sodium <2.3g/day, saturated fat <7% calories
- Physical activity: ≥150 min/week moderate aerobic exercise (walking, cycling)
- Weight management: Target BMI 18.5–24.9; even 5–10% weight loss improves risk
- Smoking cessation: Single most impactful intervention; risk decreases dramatically within 1–2 years
- Limit alcohol: ≤1 drink/day (women), ≤2 drinks/day (men)
- Stress management: Meditation, cognitive behavioral therapy, yoga

### Medications
| Drug Class | Example | Indication |
|-----------|---------|------------|
| Statins | Atorvastatin, Rosuvastatin | Dyslipidemia, CVD prevention |
| Beta-blockers | Metoprolol, Carvedilol | Post-MI, heart failure, hypertension, arrhythmia |
| ACE inhibitors | Lisinopril, Ramipril | Heart failure, post-MI, hypertension, diabetic nephropathy |
| ARBs | Valsartan, Losartan | ACE intolerant patients |
| Antiplatelet | Aspirin, Clopidogrel | Post-MI, ACS, stent |
| Anticoagulants | Warfarin, Apixaban | AFib, DVT, mechanical valves |
| Nitrates | Nitroglycerin, Isosorbide | Angina relief |
| Loop diuretics | Furosemide | Heart failure, fluid overload |
| SGLT-2 inhibitors | Dapagliflozin, Empagliflozin | Heart failure with or without diabetes |

### Interventional and Surgical Procedures
- **Percutaneous Coronary Intervention (PCI)**: Balloon angioplasty + stent placement for CAD/MI
- **Coronary Artery Bypass Grafting (CABG)**: Surgical bypass for multivessel disease
- **Implantable Cardioverter Defibrillator (ICD)**: For ventricular arrhythmias, sudden cardiac death prevention
- **Cardiac Resynchronization Therapy (CRT)**: For heart failure with bundle branch block
- **Ablation**: For arrhythmias (AFib, SVT, VT)
- **Transcatheter Aortic Valve Replacement (TAVR)**: For severe aortic stenosis in high-risk surgical patients

## Prevention

### Primary Prevention (No Known CVD)
- Assess 10-year ASCVD risk using Pooled Cohort Equations
- Statin therapy if 10-year risk ≥7.5% or LDL ≥190 mg/dL
- Blood pressure control (<130/80 mmHg target)
- Aspirin only if benefit clearly outweighs bleeding risk (no longer routine for primary prevention)
- Diabetes management; achieve HbA1c <7%

### Secondary Prevention (Established CVD)
- High-intensity statin therapy (atorvastatin 40–80mg)
- Dual antiplatelet therapy post-ACS/PCI (aspirin + P2Y12 inhibitor for 12 months)
- ACE inhibitor or ARB
- Beta-blocker post-MI for ≥3 years
- Annual influenza vaccine
- Cardiac rehabilitation program

## When to Seek Emergency Care
- Sudden severe chest pain, pressure, or tightness lasting >5 minutes
- Chest pain with sweating, nausea, shortness of breath, or radiation to arm/jaw
- Sudden severe shortness of breath at rest
- Sudden collapse or loss of consciousness
- Palpitations with dizziness or near-fainting
- Call emergency services (911) immediately — do not drive yourself
