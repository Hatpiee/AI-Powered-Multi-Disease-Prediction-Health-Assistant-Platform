# Diabetes: Complete Health Guide

## Overview
Diabetes mellitus is a chronic metabolic disease characterized by elevated blood glucose levels due to insufficient insulin production, ineffective insulin action, or both. It affects over 537 million adults worldwide and is a leading cause of cardiovascular disease, blindness, kidney failure, and lower-limb amputation.

## Types of Diabetes

### Type 1 Diabetes
- Autoimmune destruction of pancreatic beta cells leads to absolute insulin deficiency
- Typically onset in childhood or young adulthood, but can occur at any age
- Requires lifelong insulin therapy
- Accounts for 5–10% of all diabetes cases
- Associated with genetic markers (HLA-DR3, HLA-DR4) and environmental triggers

### Type 2 Diabetes
- Characterized by insulin resistance and progressive beta-cell failure
- Accounts for 90–95% of all diabetes cases
- Strongly associated with obesity, physical inactivity, and aging
- Often preventable or delayable through lifestyle modification
- Managed with oral medications, injectables, and eventually insulin

### Gestational Diabetes Mellitus (GDM)
- Develops during pregnancy due to placental hormones causing insulin resistance
- Increases risk of preeclampsia and cesarean delivery
- Elevated risk of developing Type 2 diabetes later in life for both mother and child
- Managed with dietary changes, exercise, and insulin if necessary

### Prediabetes
- Fasting glucose 100–125 mg/dL or HbA1c 5.7–6.4%
- Affects about 1 in 3 adults; most are undiagnosed
- Reversible through lifestyle intervention

## Risk Factors

### Non-Modifiable
- Age ≥45 years
- Family history of diabetes (first-degree relative)
- Race/ethnicity: African American, Hispanic, Native American, Asian American have higher risk
- History of gestational diabetes or delivering a baby >9 lbs
- Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)

### Modifiable
- Overweight or obesity (BMI ≥25, or ≥23 for Asian Americans)
- Physical inactivity (less than 150 minutes/week of moderate activity)
- Unhealthy diet (high in refined carbohydrates, sugar-sweetened beverages, processed foods)
- Smoking (increases risk by 30–40%)
- High blood pressure (≥130/80 mmHg)
- Dyslipidemia: HDL <35 mg/dL or triglycerides >250 mg/dL
- Sleep apnea and chronic sleep deprivation

## Symptoms

### Classic Symptoms (often more prominent in Type 1)
- Polydipsia (excessive thirst)
- Polyuria (frequent urination, especially at night)
- Polyphagia (excessive hunger)
- Unexplained weight loss
- Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA) in Type 1: fruity breath, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain

### Common Symptoms (Type 2, often subtle)
- Fatigue and low energy
- Blurred vision
- Slow-healing wounds or frequent infections
- Tingling, numbness, or pain in hands or feet (neuropathy)
- Recurrent skin, gum, or bladder infections
- Darkened skin in body creases (acanthosis nigricans) indicating insulin resistance

## Diagnostic Criteria (ADA Guidelines)
| Test | Normal | Prediabetes | Diabetes |
|------|--------|-------------|----------|
| Fasting Plasma Glucose | <100 mg/dL | 100–125 mg/dL | ≥126 mg/dL |
| 2-Hour OGTT (75g) | <140 mg/dL | 140–199 mg/dL | ≥200 mg/dL |
| HbA1c | <5.7% | 5.7–6.4% | ≥6.5% |
| Random Glucose + symptoms | — | — | ≥200 mg/dL |

## Monitoring and Key Lab Values
- **HbA1c target**: <7.0% for most adults (reflects 3-month average blood glucose)
- **Self-monitoring blood glucose (SMBG)**: Fasting 80–130 mg/dL; Post-meal <180 mg/dL
- **Urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio (UACR)**: Annual screening for nephropathy; >30 mg/g is abnormal
- **eGFR**: Kidney function; <60 mL/min/1.73m² indicates CKD
- **Lipid panel**: LDL <100 mg/dL (or <70 mg/dL with CVD); triglycerides <150 mg/dL
- **Blood pressure**: Target <130/80 mmHg
- **Annual eye exam**: Dilated fundus exam for diabetic retinopathy
- **Annual foot exam**: Monofilament test, pulses, inspection

## Complications

### Microvascular
- **Retinopathy**: Leading cause of new adult blindness; nonproliferative → proliferative stages
- **Nephropathy**: Leading cause of end-stage renal disease (ESRD); starts as microalbuminuria
- **Neuropathy**: Peripheral (stocking-glove) and autonomic (gastroparesis, orthostatic hypotension)

### Macrovascular
- Coronary artery disease (2–4× higher risk)
- Stroke (1.5–3× higher risk)
- Peripheral arterial disease (PAD), leading to ulcers and amputations

### Acute Complications
- **Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)**: Primarily Type 1; glucose >250 mg/dL, pH <7.3, ketones in urine
- **Hyperosmolar Hyperglycemic State (HHS)**: Primarily Type 2; glucose often >600 mg/dL, severe dehydration
- **Hypoglycemia**: Glucose <70 mg/dL; caused by excess insulin, missed meals, exercise

## Management

### Lifestyle Modifications
- **Weight loss**: 5–7% body weight reduction reduces Type 2 diabetes risk by 58%
- **Physical activity**: ≥150 min/week moderate aerobic activity + resistance training 2×/week
- **Diet**: Mediterranean diet, DASH diet, or low-carbohydrate diet; limit refined carbs, sugar-sweetened beverages, saturated fats
- **Smoking cessation**: Reduces cardiovascular risk significantly

### Medications for Type 2 Diabetes
| Drug Class | Example | Mechanism | Notes |
|-----------|---------|-----------|-------|
| Biguanides | Metformin | Reduces hepatic glucose production | First-line; cardioprotective |
| SGLT-2 inhibitors | Empagliflozin | Glucosuria | Heart/kidney protective |
| GLP-1 agonists | Semaglutide | Increases insulin, reduces appetite | Weight loss, CV benefit |
| DPP-4 inhibitors | Sitagliptin | Increases incretin | Weight neutral |
| Sulfonylureas | Glipizide | Stimulates insulin secretion | Risk of hypoglycemia |
| Insulin | Various types | Replaces/supplements insulin | Required for Type 1 |

### Type 1 Diabetes Management
- Multiple daily injections (MDI) or continuous subcutaneous insulin infusion (CSII/pump)
- Rapid-acting (lispro, aspart) + long-acting (glargine, detemir) insulin
- Continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) systems recommended
- Carbohydrate counting and insulin-to-carb ratios

## Prevention
- **Primary prevention**: Weight loss, exercise, healthy diet for high-risk individuals
- **Diabetes Prevention Program (DPP)**: Structured lifestyle program reduces progression from prediabetes by 58%; metformin reduces by 31%
- **Screening recommendations**: Every 3 years for adults ≥35 with risk factors; annually if prediabetes

## When to Seek Medical Attention
- Blood glucose persistently >250 mg/dL
- Symptoms of DKA: vomiting, abdominal pain, fruity breath
- Severe hypoglycemia (glucose <54 mg/dL or unconsciousness)
- New or worsening symptoms of complications (vision changes, foot ulcers, chest pain)
- HbA1c consistently above target despite medication adherence
